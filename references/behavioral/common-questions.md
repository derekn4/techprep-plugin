# Common Behavioral Questions

A reference list of behavioral prompts to practice your STAR stories against. Grouped by theme. Most real interview questions are variations of these.

## Conflict

- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a coworker. How did you resolve it?
- Describe a situation where you had to work with a difficult teammate.
- Tell me about a time your manager made a decision you disagreed with.
- How do you handle pushback on your ideas?

## Failure

- Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?
- Describe a project that didn't go as planned.
- When was the last time you made a significant mistake? What did you do?
- Tell me about a time you received critical feedback.

## Ownership

- Tell me about a project you drove end-to-end.
- Describe a time you went beyond your assigned responsibilities.
- Tell me about a time you identified a problem no one else noticed.
- Describe something you built or improved that wasn't asked for.

## Ambiguity

- Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.
- Describe a project with unclear requirements. How did you proceed?
- How do you handle a situation with no clear owner?

## Technical tradeoffs

- Tell me about a technical decision you made with significant tradeoffs.
- Describe a time you chose a less-than-ideal solution. Why?
- Tell me about a time you argued against "the best" solution.

## Leadership / influence (senior+)

- Tell me about a time you led a project without formal authority.
- Describe a time you influenced a decision across teams.
- How did you get buy-in for a significant change?
- Tell me about a time you mentored someone.

## Mentorship (mid+)

- Describe a time you helped a junior engineer grow.
- Tell me about code review feedback you gave that someone pushed back on.
- Have you ever onboarded someone to your team?

## Scope change / adaptability

- Tell me about a time your project's requirements changed mid-flight.
- Describe a time you had to drop features to meet a deadline.
- How do you handle a sudden shift in priorities?

## Cross-functional

- Describe a time you worked closely with non-engineers (PM, design, legal, etc.).
- Tell me about a disagreement between engineering and product. How was it resolved?
- How do you translate technical constraints to non-technical stakeholders?

## Tight deadline

- Tell me about a time you had to deliver under significant time pressure.
- Describe a time you had to cut scope. How did you decide what to keep?
- How do you handle competing urgent priorities?

## Growth / self-awareness

- What's a weakness you're actively working on?
- How do you decide what to learn next?
- What's the most recent skill you picked up?
- Where do you see yourself in 2–3 years?

## Motivation

- Why do you want to work here?
- Why are you looking to leave your current role?
- What's the most interesting problem you've worked on recently?
- What's an accomplishment you're proud of?

---

## Practice approach

1. Pick a question.
2. Map it to a story from your repertoire (see `story-template.md`).
3. Tell the story out loud. Record yourself or use `/tech-coach:behavioral` for live coaching.
4. Time it. Aim for 2–3 minutes.
5. Ask yourself the probing follow-ups listed in the story template.

If no story from your repertoire fits a question, that's a gap — draft a new story for that theme.
