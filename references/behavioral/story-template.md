# STAR Story Template

Fill this in for each story in your repertoire. Aim for 4–6 distinct stories covering different themes (see `star-methodology.md` for the theme list). One story can often be reframed to answer multiple question types.

---

## Story title: _______

**Theme(s):** (conflict / failure / ownership / ambiguity / tradeoff / leadership / mentorship / scope-change / cross-functional / tight-deadline)

**One-line summary:** (the hook — what an interviewer hears in the first 10 seconds)

---

### Situation

- When: _______
- Where (team/project/company): _______
- What was happening: _______

(2–3 sentences max in the actual telling.)

### Task

- Your specific role: _______
- The problem you personally owned: _______

### Action

List the key decisions and actions you took. This is the meat — be specific:

1. _______
2. _______
3. _______

For each, note:
- Why you chose it over alternatives
- What tradeoff it involved
- What you'd do differently now (if anything)

### Result

- What changed: _______
- Metrics (if any): _______
- What you learned: _______

## Self-check before rehearsing

- [ ] Can I tell this in 2–3 minutes out loud?
- [ ] Do I use "I" more than "we" in the Action section?
- [ ] Is there a real decision point with tradeoffs, not just a sequence of events?
- [ ] Do I end with a concrete outcome, not a vague "it went well"?
- [ ] Does this story match the **level** I'm interviewing for? (See methodology doc.)

## Probing questions to anticipate

Interviewers rarely accept the first answer. Be ready for:
- "What was the hardest part?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- "How did your teammate react?"
- "What did your manager think?"
- "How did you measure success?"
- "What was the alternative you rejected, and why?"

Write one-line answers for each *before* the interview. Don't improvise under pressure.
