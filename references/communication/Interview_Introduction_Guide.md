# Interview Introduction Guide - "Tell Me About Yourself"

## The 60-Second Introduction for Technical Screens

### Purpose
First impression that establishes your qualifications and sets the tone. Keep it **concise, relevant, and professional**.

---

## ✅ Optimized Introduction (60 seconds / ~120 words)

**"Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm a Software Engineer with [N] years of [specialty] experience and a background in [domain]. I got into programming [brief origin story - 1 line] and then focused on [area] through [degree/early career].**

**Currently at [Company], I build [type of applications] with [primary stack]. Some highlights: I built [project 1 with metric], developed [project 2 with metric], and optimized [project 3 with metric].**

**What excites me about this role is working on [product/area] - [one specific thing that genuinely interests you]. That kind of [challenge type] really appeals to me."**

---

## 📋 Structure Breakdown

### 1. Who You Are (1 sentence)
- Role + experience level + specialty
- Example: "I'm a Software Engineer with [N] years of full-stack experience and a background in [specialty]"

### 2. Background (1-2 sentences)
- Brief origin story - why/when you got into tech
- Keep it interesting but short
- Example: "I got into programming early - [memorable hook] - then focused on [area] through [degree/path]"

### 3. Current Work (2-3 sentences)
- Company name + what you do
- 2-3 concrete achievements with metrics
- Choose achievements relevant to the role
- Example: "Currently at [Company], I build [type of applications]... [specific achievements]"

### 4. Why This Role (1-2 sentences)
- Connect your experience to the position
- Show genuine interest
- Example: "What excites me about this role is working on [product/area] - [one specific thing that genuinely interests you]"

**Total Time: 45-60 seconds**

---

## 🎯 Alternative: Ultra-Short Version (30 seconds)

Use this if you sense they want to move quickly or if they've already read your resume:

**"Sure! I'm [Name], a Software Engineer with [N] years of [specialty] experience. Currently at [Company] building [project type] and optimizing [system]. I have a [degree] from [school]. I'm excited about [product/area] because I enjoy [type of work]."**

Then let them ask follow-ups if they want more detail.

---

## Key Achievements to Highlight (Choose 2-3)

Pick 2-3 achievements from your resume that are most relevant to the target role. Prioritize achievements that:

- Include a **specific, quantified metric** (percentage, scale, throughput, latency)
- Map directly to the skills the job description calls out
- You can confidently deep-dive on if asked a follow-up

### Full-Stack / Systems Roles:
Pick achievements showing end-to-end delivery, performance optimization, or cross-layer work (frontend + backend + DB).

### Performance / Scale Roles:
Pick achievements involving high-throughput systems, database/cache tuning, concurrency, or distributed systems.

### ML / AI Roles:
Pick achievements involving model training, eval metrics (BLEU, perplexity, accuracy), data pipelines, or inference serving.

---

## ✅ DO:

1. **Keep it under 90 seconds** (aim for 60)
2. **Use specific metrics** (75% reduction, 35,000+ URLs, 10,000+ connections)
3. **Connect your background to the role** (show you understand what they need)
4. **Sound enthusiastic but professional** (genuine interest, not over-eager)
5. **Practice out loud** (5+ times before interview)
6. **End with why you're interested** (shows motivation)

---

## ❌ DON'T:

1. **Don't tell your life story** - This isn't a biography
2. **Don't list every project** - Choose 2-3 most relevant
3. **Don't use filler phrases** - Avoid "you know," "kind of," "actually," "like"
4. **Don't apologize or undersell** - No "just," "only," "I'm still learning"
5. **Don't be too casual** - "I end up building whatever" → "I build full-stack applications"
6. **Don't memorize word-for-word** - Know the structure, speak naturally
7. **Don't go over 90 seconds** - Respect their time

---

## What to CUT from Long Introductions

Common things that make intros too long:

❌ **Remove:**
- "Being at a startup, I end up building whatever needs building" (too casual, vague)
- Detailed bullet points (save for "Tell me about a project")
- "I've also done extensive data work for my ML projects" (only if directly relevant)
- Filler phrases: "And you know," "kind of thing," "actually"
- Self-assessment: "I think I'm a good fit because..." (let them decide)
- Multiple examples per category (pick the strongest 1-2)

---

## Practice Protocol

### Before Interview Day:

1. **Write out your 60-second version**
2. **Read it out loud 5 times** (not in your head!)
3. **Time yourself** - Should be 45-60 seconds
4. **Record yourself** (optional but helpful)
5. **Practice without notes** - Know the flow, not exact words

### Day Before Interview:

1. **Practice 2-3 times**
2. **Adjust based on the specific role** (emphasize relevant achievements)
3. **Don't over-rehearse** - Stay natural

### Day of Interview:

1. **Review your key points** (not the full script)
2. **Remember: 60 seconds max**
3. **Breathe and speak clearly**

---

## Tailoring for Different Roles

Match what you emphasize to the target role's technical focus:

### For Systems / Platform Engineering:
Emphasize:
- Cross-platform or low-level development experience
- Performance optimization (DB, caching, concurrency)
- Full-stack capabilities
- Any prior work on the specific platform/product they're building

### For Backend / Infrastructure:
Emphasize:
- Systems-language work (C++, Rust, Go) if relevant
- Database performance (concurrency, query tuning)
- Distributed systems (caching, messaging, replication)
- API architecture

### For Full-Stack Web:
Emphasize:
- Frontend framework experience (React, Vue, etc.)
- Browser-facing products you've shipped
- CI/CD pipelines
- RESTful or GraphQL API development

### For ML / AI:
Emphasize:
- Relevant graduate coursework or degree
- Production ML work (training, eval, serving)
- Model-specific metrics (BLEU, perplexity, accuracy)
- Computer vision, NLP, or GenAI projects

---

## Common Follow-up Questions After Introduction

Be ready for:

1. **"Tell me more about [specific project]"**
   - Have 2-3 minute deep dive ready for each achievement you mentioned
   - Include: Problem, Your Approach, Technical Details, Impact

2. **"Why are you interested in this role?"**
   - Connect your experience to their needs
   - Show you understand what the role entails
   - Mention specific aspects that excite you

3. **"What are you looking for in your next role?"**
   - Growth opportunities
   - Technical challenges
   - Team collaboration
   - Align with what they offer

4. **"Walk me through your resume"**
   - Chronological overview (2-3 min)
   - Focus on progression and key achievements
   - End with why you're here today
